La Trappiste

The crowd quiets down to allow a single pair of lips to part

in the formation of a poem. Drunk on Belgian beers,

attentive ears are eager for what one voice will impart.



Canterbury cheers for the sound of such black art.

French, Italian, English, American offer their scripts when

the crowd quiets down to allow a single pair of lips to part.



Students bundled in coats and notebooks depart

stifling rooms for cobblestone roads. In a small café

attentive ears are eager for what one voice will impart.



For one, a snow covered Cathedral inspires a work of art.

Gloved, shaking hands don’t stop the scribbling of pens.

The crowd quiets down to allow a single pair of lips to part.



Sometimes we pretend to be Tolkein, Michelangelo, or Descartes.

In the café, we envy other’s prose and poetry, all the while

attentive ears are eager for what one voice will impart.



If you find me lost, look no further, I’ll tell you to start

in this Belgain café, in a town where history has stopped,

where the crowd quiets down to allow a single pair of lips to part,

and attentive ears are eager for what one voice will impart.
